The 5 Biggest July 4th Insurance Myths That Could Ruin Your Summer

Myth #1: "All Fire Damage Is Automatically Covered"

WRONG. Many policies have specific exclusions for:

  • Grills placed too close to structures (usually within 10 feet)
  • Unattended cooking fires
  • Damage from illegal fireworks
  • Fire spread due to "negligence"

Last month, I helped the Martinez family in Elkin after their insurance company denied a $22,000 claim because their grill was 8 feet from their house instead of the required 10 feet. We found them a new policy with Travelers that actually protects them.

Myth #2: "Fireworks Damage Is Always Covered"

Here's what the big insurance companies don't advertise: If fireworks are illegal in your area and you use them anyway, your claim can be denied.

In many NC counties, certain fireworks are prohibited. If your neighbor's illegal firework lands on your roof and starts a fire, YOUR insurance covers it. But if YOU set off the illegal firework? You might be on your own.

Myth #3: "My Liability Coverage Protects Me at Parties"

Think your liability insurance covers everything at your July 4th party? Think again. Standard policies often exclude:

  • Injuries from trampolines or pools without proper safety equipment
  • Dog bites if your dog has any history of aggression
  • Alcohol-related incidents (yes, really)
  • Injuries from fireworks you provided
